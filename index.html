WHO I AM

Professional Profile

My degree is in applied mathematics, and my thesis is the first formal study to produce closed-form probabilistic results for blockchain consensus dynamics, covering fork formation, chain reorganization, and adversarial attack scenarios under both PoW and PoS using Phase Type distributions, in a domain where no such framework previously existed. That background changes how I reason about protocol security and system design: I bring first-principles mathematical thinking to problems most practitioners treat as empirical or intuitive. Blockchain protocols are what I find most technically interesting, and I build in this space by choice outside of work; Concord and Obsignata are both deployed to production. ACADEMIC BACKGROUND

Education

Bachelor's in Applied Mathematics

Faculty of Sciences · UNAM · Mexico City · 2020–2025
GPA: 9.36 / 10.0

Thesis: Stochastic Model of the Consensus Process Dynamics in the Blockchain using Population Genetics Tools

The first study of its kind to produce formal analytical results for blockchain consensus dynamics, a domain previously lacking closed-form probabilistic descriptions. Develops models using Phase Type distributions, capturing the full dynamics of fork formation, chain reorganization, and adversarial attack scenarios under both Proof of Work and Proof of Stake. Core contributions include closed-form expressions for the probability of successful attacks and quantitative characterization of network vulnerability as a function of adversarial hash power or stake.
